Burger

Description

A burger is a sandwich made with a patty of cooked ground meat between two halves of a bun. Burgers are an American fast food that are traditionally made with ground beef. They are often served with toppings like lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, ketchup, mustard, bacon, avocado, mushrooms, cheese sauce, or chili.

Ingredients

  • sesame Bun
  • ground beef
  • Tomato
  • American Cheese
  • Condiments

Steps

  1. Form the beef into a ball, then smash it into a disc
  2. Use your thumb to make a small indentation in the center of each patty. This helps the burger cook evenly and prevents it from puffing up while cooking.
  3. Put the buns face down in a skillet for a few seconds to toast the inside so they don't get soggy when they touch the patty's fat.
  4. Grill the burgers at about 300Â°F. Grilling gives the burgers a delicious crust and a better texture than pressing them.
  5. Grill the burgers on very high heat so the fat caramelizes and forms a crispy exterior while the burger sears.
